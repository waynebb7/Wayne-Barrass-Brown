Buying a Metro Ticket in Tokyo

Disclaimer: This is entirely fictional. Any resemblance to actual ticket machines in Tokyo or any other city is purely coincidental.

Every station on the Tokyo Metro system will have a ticket vending machines. Here’s how to use them:

  1. Select the language you wish to use.

  2. Select the type of pass you want. If you are going to be in Tokyo for more than three days, we recommend a weekly pass; the convenience of not having to buy a new ticket every day outweighs the extra cost.

  3. Select the number of adult and child tickets you want, using the arrows to increase or decrease the number. Children are people from age 2 to 15; children below the age of 2 ride free.

  4. Finally, insert payment. Coins and bills go in on the left; you may use a debit card or credit card in the slot at the right. Tickets are dispensed in the bin at the bottom of the payment area.
